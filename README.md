# Sivram Sahu

**Founder/operator building Confer AI Solutions as an AI-first company.**

I build practical AI systems that move work from "someone should remember to do this" to "a trusted agent owns the loop." My current focus is agentic operations: reliable AI teammates that can take delegated work, remember commitments, follow up over time, and escalate cleanly when human judgment is needed.

## What I'm building

**Confer AI Solutions** is my work toward an AI-first company: not a business that occasionally uses AI, but one where agents become part of the operating system for daily execution.

The thesis is simple:

- People should be able to delegate work to AI the way they delegate to a capable teammate.
- Recurring reminders and follow-through should be handled by systems, not memory.
- Internal operations should become visible, auditable, and easier to improve.
- Automation should stay safe through clear scope, review gates, and human oversight.

## Current focus

- **Hermes / OpenClaw-style agents** — long-running agents with tools, memory, scheduling, messaging, and escalation paths.
- **Employee task delegation** — scoped handoffs, accountable execution, progress updates, and reviewed results.
- **Recurring reminders & operations loops** — systems that keep commitments from falling through the cracks.
- **Internal automation** — reducing repetitive back-office work so teams can spend more time on judgment and relationships.
- **Applied AI products** — document understanding, healthcare text analytics, anomaly detection, and decision-support tools.

## Working principles

- Build useful systems, not demos.
- Prefer small, reliable workflows over flashy automation that cannot be trusted.
- Keep humans in control of scope, approval, and judgment.
- Make agent work auditable: clear task ownership, durable handoffs, and visible outcomes.
- Treat AI-first operations as a company design problem, not just a tooling problem.
